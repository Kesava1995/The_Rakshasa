**Chapter 6: The Revelation**

Exactly a month had peacefully passed since Advik and Chetana\'s
unforgettable first date. The fond memory of that beautiful evening
still had a remarkable way of appearing in Advik\'s mind at the most
unexpected moments. Sometimes, it was the vivid recollection of Chetana
blushing softly in her elegant saree. Other times, it was the fleeting
sensation of the quick, tender kiss she had boldly planted on his cheek
right before rushing into her house. Most often, however, it was simply
the profound, heartwarming realization that someone genuinely cared
about him. For a Rakshasa living hidden among ordinary humans,
experiencing that deep, unconditional feeling was surprisingly precious.

His phone vibrated on his desk, and Advik glanced at the screen,
immediately smiling when he saw Chetana\'s name. He answered warmly,
\"Hello.\". \"Hello,\" Chetana replied, but there was something
suspiciously, almost dangerously cheerful in her voice. Advik narrowed
his eyes, knowing perfectly well that whenever she sounded exactly like
that, she was undoubtedly planning something mischievous.

\"What happened?\" he asked cautiously.

\"What do you mean?\" she deflected innocently.

\"You sound dangerous,\" he pointed out.

\"Dangerous?\" she repeated, and after a brief, telling silence, she
laughed brightly. \"Thank you.\".

\"That wasn\'t a compliment,\" Advik sighed.

\"I\'ll take it as one,\" she countered happily. Advik shook his head,
muttering that he should have known better. \"Good. Then listen
carefully,\" Chetana instructed. \"I\'m listening,\" he assured her.

\"I want you to take me somewhere this evening,\" she announced.

Advik leaned back comfortably in his chair. \"Somewhere?\" he echoed.

\"Yes,\" she confirmed.

\"What kind of somewhere?\" he pressed.

\"Somewhere secluded,\" she stated plainly. Advik blinked in surprise,
and for a long moment, complete, stunned silence filled his apartment.
Then, a wicked grin slowly appeared on his face. \"A secluded place?\"
he clarified.

\"Yes,\" she repeated.

\"Just the two of us?\".

\"Yes,\" she said again.

His grin widened mischievously. \"Oh.\". \"Chetana\...\" he began, his
tone teasing.

\"What?\".

\"I think I understand,\" he chuckled.

\"No, you don\'t,\" she shot back immediately.

\"I definitely understand,\" he insisted confidently.

\"No, you don\'t,\" she repeated firmly.

\"I absolutely understand,\" he teased again.

There was a heavy pause on the line before she finally spoke again.
\"Advik,\" she said sharply.

\"Hm?\".

\"Stop overthinking,\" she ordered. His grin immediately vanished into a
pout. \"Damn,\" he muttered in defeat. Chetana laughed again, clearly
triumphant. \"I knew exactly what was going on inside your head,\" she
gloated.

\"I feel unfairly judged,\" he complained.

\"You should,\" she replied without hesitation. Conceding the match,
Advik sighed. \"Fine. Then where do you want to go?\".

\"You choose,\" she offered.

\"That\'s a terrible idea,\" he immediately protested.

\"Why?\" she asked.

\"Because now if the place is bad, you\'ll blame me,\" he reasoned.

\"I won\'t,\" she promised.

\"You absolutely will,\" he insisted.

\"I won\'t,\" she maintained.

\"You absolutely will,\" he repeated.

\"I won\'t,\" she promised once more.

Advik sighed dramatically, declaring, \"I am surrounded by lies.\".
Ignoring his theatrics, she finalized the plan. \"Pick me up at five.\".
\"Yes, ma\'am,\" he agreed obediently. \"Good,\" she said, and the call
abruptly disconnected. Advik stared at his phone for a moment before he
chuckled to himself. Musing over the idea of a secluded place, he shook
his head in amusement. A few moments later, he opened his laptop and
diligently began searching for picturesque locations outside the city
limits. Eventually, he found a beautiful hilltop viewpoint nearly an
hour away that promised to be quiet, scenic, and perfectly far from the
bustling crowds.

At exactly five in the evening, Chetana stepped out of her house,
looking absolutely breathtaking. Advik instantly forgot whatever clever
greeting he was about to say; for several long seconds, he could do
nothing but simply stare at her. Chetana folded her arms, raising an
expectant eyebrow. \"What?\" she prompted.

\"You look unfair,\" he breathed honestly.

\"What does that even mean?\" she asked, amused.

\"It means everyone else should just stop trying,\" he explained
smoothly. She rolled her eyes affectionately.

\"You are impossible,\" she told him.

\"Thank you,\" he grinned.

\"That wasn\'t a compliment,\" she reminded him.

\"I\'ll take it as one,\" he fired back, mirroring her earlier words as
she laughed and entered the car.

The drive was incredibly pleasant as the dense city slowly disappeared
behind them, with towering buildings giving way to open roads, and those
open roads eventually giving way to rolling hills. By the time they
finally reached the designated viewpoint, the vast evening sky had
already begun turning a brilliant shade of orange. The elevated hilltop
overlooked a wide, sweeping stretch of land, and far away, the tiny,
twinkling lights from remote villages were just beginning to appear in
the dusk. A cool, refreshing breeze swept across the hill as they stood
together in comfortable silence for several seconds, simply watching the
majestic sunset.

\"It is beautiful,\" Chetana said softly, mesmerized by the view.

Advik glanced warmly at her profile. \"Yes,\" he agreed.

She looked back at him, a knowing smile playing on her lips. \"You
weren\'t looking at the sunset,\" she observed.

\"No,\" he admitted shamelessly.

Her smile widened. \"I noticed,\" she said.

\"I know,\" he replied softly. They spread a comfortable mat on the
grassy ground and unpacked the simple dinner they had brought along. The
meal itself was entirely ordinary, yet somehow, sitting there together,
everything tasted infinitely better. Perhaps it was because they were
sharing it, or perhaps it was simply because neither of them wanted the
perfect evening to end. Time passed easily as their conversation drifted
effortlessly from work complaints to childhood memories, embarrassing
office gossip, and their future plans. For a beautiful, uninterrupted
while, nothing else in the world existed except the two of them.

Suddenly, the harsh, grating sound of revving motorcycles violently
broke the peaceful silence. Advik\'s relaxed expression changed
immediately. Three bikes roared onto the hilltop, carrying six young men
in their early twenties who were loud, visibly drunk, and clearly
looking for trouble. The bikes aggressively stopped nearby, and the
unruly men noticed Chetana almost instantly. Advik quietly placed his
water bottle down on the mat. One of the men whistled vulgarly, another
laughed crudely, and a third swaggered closer to their spot. Any trace
of a smile had already completely disappeared from Advik\'s face. He
rose slowly and deliberately to his feet. Chetana noticed the chilling
change in his demeanor immediately.

The approaching man offered a sinister grin. \"Brother,\" he mocked.
Advik said nothing. The man\'s grin widened as he leered. \"Nice
company,\" he sneered, making the others laugh loudly. Chetana\'s
expression hardened with disgust as the man boldly took another step
forward.

Advik finally spoke, his voice remaining terrifyingly calm and level.
\"Leave,\" he commanded.

The man blinked in mock surprise. \"What?\" he asked.

\"Leave,\" Advik repeated clearly.

The raucous laughter abruptly stopped, and for a tense moment, the
entire group simply stared at him. Then, one of them laughed loudly,
quickly joined by another, until soon all of them were laughing
mockingly at the solitary man making demands. The leader, looking deeply
amused, stepped up. \"Or what?\" he challenged.

Advik\'s piercing eyes remained fixed entirely on him. \"Or you leave
unhappy,\" he stated flatly.

The leader\'s grin vanished instantly, and the atmosphere on the hilltop
fundamentally changed. It wasn\'t a dramatic or immediate shift, but it
was enough that Chetana could vividly feel it in the air; something
highly dangerous beneath Advik\'s calmness had begun to move and awaken.
The man provocatively took another step forward.

Advik\'s eyes narrowed dangerously. \"Last warning,\" he cautioned.

The man laughed dismissively. \"Or what?\" he repeated. One of his
cronies pointed disrespectfully at Chetana. \"Maybe we should ask madam
what she thinks,\" he suggested, prompting another loud burst of
laughter. The group confidently began moving closer---not quite enough
to launch an outright attack, but just enough to forcefully intimidate
and make their malicious intentions clear.

Advik remained completely motionless, like a coiled spring. Chetana
looked at him and instantly knew something was wrong; however, the
wrongness wasn\'t with the aggressive men, but rather with Advik
himself. His expression had become unnaturally still and almost
frighteningly cold. The arrogant leader noticed this chilling stillness
too, but unfortunately for him, he fatally interpreted it as human fear.

Smirking, the leader taunted, \"You know, you should learn when to mind
your own business.\".

Advik exhaled a long, slow breath. \"I am trying very hard to be
reasonable,\" he warned quietly.

The leader scoffed loudly. \"Reasonable?\" he mocked.

\"Yes,\" Advik confirmed.

The man arrogantly stepped forward once again. \"So what happens if we
don\'t leave?\" he demanded.

For a brief, ominous moment, Advik closed his eyes. When he opened them
again, there was something deeply unsettling swirling within them---it
wasn\'t exactly anger, but something far older and far more predatory.
\"You really don\'t want to know,\" Advik told him truthfully.

Seeking to provoke him further, the leader shoved Advik lightly on the
shoulder. While the physical gesture was not particularly strong, and to
a normal human it would have been little more than a petty insult, to
Advik, it was the absolute final mistake. Absolute silence fell over the
hill. The evening breeze suddenly felt perceptibly colder, and a
strange, primal unease crawled sharply up Chetana\'s spine as the
ignorant men continued their foolish laughing. None of them noticed that
Advik\'s hands had slowly curled into tight fists, nor did they realize
that his rhythm of breathing had fundamentally changed. Crucially, none
of them noticed the faint, unnatural golden glow that was just beginning
to appear deep within his eyes.

Finally, one of the goons did notice. His arrogant smile disappeared
instantly. \"Bro\...\" he stammered nervously. The others ignored him,
continuing their taunts. \"Bro\...\" the young man repeated, pointing a
trembling finger directly at Advik. \"What?\" the leader snapped.
Everyone turned to follow the pointed finger, and instantly, they all
froze in absolute horror.

The ominous glow in Advik\'s eyes had become entirely unmistakable---a
bright, inhuman gold that burned with ancient power. The leader took a
sudden, involuntary step backward as, for the very first time, genuine
uncertainty appeared on his pale face. \"What the hell\...\" he
whispered.

When Advik finally spoke, his voice sounded completely wrong; it was far
deeper, much rougher, and sounded more like a guttural growl than human
speech. \"I asked you to leave,\" the creature rumbled.

The group merely stared, paralyzed as true fear rapidly began to replace
their drunken confidence. One of them tried to laugh it off nervously,
stammering, \"He\'s doing some kind of trick,\" but absolutely nobody
sounded convinced by the weak lie. The golden glow intensified fiercely.
Massive muscles rapidly expanded beneath Advik\'s clothes, his
underlying bones shifted with sickening crunches, and a low, terrifying
growl emerged from somewhere deep within his expanding chest. The
guttural sound did not resemble anything remotely human, causing
Chetana\'s eyes to widen in shock. The terrified men stumbled backward,
frantically shouting, \"What is happening? What the hell is
happening?\".

Advik\'s fingers viciously lengthened, his nails sharpening into lethal
claws as his broad shoulders expanded further. The horrifying
transformation violently accelerated as the sharp sound of tearing cloth
echoed loudly across the secluded hilltop. Thick, golden fur erupted
wildly across his expanding body, his face elongated into a bestial
snout, and his jaw widened as his teeth lengthened into razor-sharp
fangs. Within mere seconds, the polite software engineer standing before
them no longer existed; in his place stood something straight from
another, long-forgotten age. It was a towering, nightmarish being---half
man, half lion---whose golden eyes burned intensely like molten fire and
whose massive claws gleamed dangerously in the fading sunlight. The
majestic creature\'s heavy mane moved gently in the evening wind as
absolutely nobody dared to move or breathe; the world itself seemed
utterly frozen in terror.

Then, the towering creature opened its massive mouth and roared. The
deafening sound exploded violently across the hill, sending flocks of
birds erupting from distant trees and making the very ground itself seem
to tremble beneath their feet. The young men nearly collapsed from the
sheer weight of the terror. One of them screamed shrilly, another fell
backward in a panic, and the leader\'s face became completely devoid of
blood. The creature took a single, deliberate step forward, and that was
all it took. Every remaining trace of false courage vanished completely.

The men ran for their lives. They scrambled desperately toward their
motorcycles, some nearly falling over in their frantic attempts to start
the engines while others abandoned their dignity entirely. Within
moments, the loud engines roared to life, and the bikes sped away down
the dark road as fast as their terrified riders could possibly manage.
None of them dared to look back.

The hilltop became eerily silent once more, leaving only the sound of
the wind sweeping across the grass. For several long seconds, the
imposing creature continued staring fiercely in the direction they had
fled. Then, slowly, the violent transformation began to reverse itself.
The golden fur disappeared, the sharp claws safely retracted, the deadly
fangs shortened, and the thick mane vanished entirely as the lion-like
face smoothed back into human features once more.

A quiet minute later, Advik stood there again, breathing heavily and
remaining completely silent. Neither he nor Chetana spoke, and the
physical distance between them suddenly felt impossibly enormous.
Chetana simply stared at him in shock, while Advik shamefully looked
away. He had anxiously imagined this exact moment many times in his
life, and in his mind, none of the possible outcomes had ever ended
well. Finally, Chetana found her voice, though her throat felt
incredibly dry.

\"What\...\" she croaked, swallowing hard. \"What was that?\".

Advik heavily closed his eyes, realizing there was absolutely no point
in hiding the truth anymore. He had spent his entire existence fiercely
protecting this ancient secret, and now, it was completely gone. Slowly,
he turned his heavy gaze toward her. \"You already know,\" he told her.

An absolute silence stretched between them as Chetana\'s mind raced back
to a profound conversation from years ago. She remembered her
knowledgeable father, the deep lessons from the Puranas, the ancient
tales of shape-shifting night creatures, and the powerful beings hidden
amongst ordinary humans. The fragmented memories returned one by one,
and her heartbeat quickened in her chest. She looked directly into his
eyes, her voice trembling slightly. \"Are you\...\" she started, feeling
the question was absurd and impossible, yet she bravely asked it anyway.
\"Are you a Rakshasa?\".

Advik held her intense gaze for a moment, then nodded slowly. \"Yes,\"
he confessed.

The heavy word landed between them like a clap of thunder, followed by a
silence that was long, heavy, and painfully thick. Advik looked away,
his heart sinking. He had bravely faced incredibly dangerous criminals
and stood his ground against creatures far stronger than any human, yet
none of those terrifying moments felt even remotely as frightening as
this singular, vulnerable one.

Finally, breaking the unbearable quiet, he spoke. \"You should know
something,\" he started, while Chetana remained perfectly silent. \"I
never wanted to lie to you,\" he explained, his voice dropping to a
quiet murmur. \"I just didn\'t know how to tell you the truth.\".

More heavy silence followed his confession.

\"If you want to leave\...\" he began, swallowing the lump in his
throat, \"\...I understand.\". Chetana looked up at him as he forced
himself to continue the painful speech. \"You don\'t owe me anything,\"
he told her, his chest feeling strangely tight with impending
heartbreak. \"You saw what I am.\". After another agonizing pause, he
managed a weak, sad smile. \"If you\'re uncomfortable\... walk away,\"
he offered generously.

Saying those words hurt him far more than he had ever expected, but he
knew they were absolutely necessary. To Advik, true love meant giving
her a genuine choice, not selfishly taking it away.

The evening wind blew softly across the quiet hilltop as Chetana simply
stared at him for several long seconds. Then, she surprisingly began
walking forward. One step. Two steps. Three. Advik remained completely
still, bracing himself for the worst; he had expected many things, such
as righteous anger, screaming fear, endless questions, or bitter tears.
What happened next had never even crossed his mind.

Chetana wrapped her arms firmly around his waist and hugged him tightly.

Advik completely froze, genuinely forgetting how to think or breathe for
a moment.

\"You idiot,\" she whispered fiercely.

His eyes widened in shock. \"What?\" he breathed.

\"You absolute idiot,\" she repeated.

\"Chetana\...\" he mumbled, entirely confused.

She stubbornly tightened her hug around him. \"I fell in love with you
before I knew any of this,\" she declared. Her honest words struck him
much harder than any physical weapon ever could. \"I know what I saw,\"
she continued, her voice trembling slightly. \"I was terrified.\". She
let out a weak, watery laugh. \"I\'m still terrified,\" she admitted.

Advik said nothing, overwhelmed by the raw emotion.

\"But\...\" she murmured, burying her face deeply against his chest,
\"\...you\'re still you.\".

At her profound acceptance, the last remaining shards of fear inside his
heart completely cracked and melted away. Slowly, and almost hesitantly,
he raised his arms and wrapped them securely around her. The brilliant
sunset finally faded entirely beyond the horizon, allowing the peaceful
night to gently settle over the hill. And for the very first time in his
long, hidden life, Advik had finally allowed another human being to know
his terrifying truth, and miraculously, she had chosen to stay.
