**Chapter 4: The First Date**

The first few weeks at Rolling Updates passed by in a rapid, demanding
blur for Chetana. Like any new corporate employee, she dedicated her
initial days to memorizing names, understanding complex workflows, and
desperately trying to not look completely confused during long meetings.
Fortunately for her, the new colleagues she met were incredibly warm and
friendly.

However, among the sea of new faces, one particular person stood out
immediately: Advik. At first, she simply found him to be a very strange
individual. While most people arrived at the office in the morning
looking exhausted and sleepy, Advik only ever looked sleepy when the
bright sun was up in the sky. When team leads assigned late-night tasks,
most employees complained bitterly, but Advik practically volunteered
for the late hours with genuine enthusiasm. Furthermore, while others
drank copious amounts of coffee just to stay awake, Advik drank it
simply because he genuinely enjoyed the taste.

One particular evening, while several employees were sitting together
during a well-deserved break, a curious colleague casually asked Advik a
question.

\"Advik, when do you actually sleep?\" the colleague asked.

Advik thought about it for a brief moment before answering. \"Whenever
the sun starts behaving suspiciously,\" he replied.

As everyone stared at him in utter confusion, Rajesh chimed in. \"What
does that even mean?\".

Shrugging casually, Advik explained, \"If it\'s daytime, I don\'t trust
it.\".

The entire break room erupted into genuine laughter, and Chetana found
herself laughing right along with them. That was the very first time she
realized she genuinely enjoyed being in his company.

As weeks gradually turned into months, the two of them slowly grew much
closer. They fell into a comfortable routine of sharing their lunch
breaks, debating passionately over movies, arguing playfully about food
preferences, and exchanging text messages late into the quiet hours of
the night. For Advik, staying awake late into the night was perfectly
normal, but for Chetana, it was a completely new experience.

One morning, she arrived at her desk yawning heavily, only to find Advik
looking perfectly energetic and alert.

\"You were awake until two?\" he asked her knowingly.

Chetana frowned. \"How do you know?\".

\"You sent me three messages at 1:47 AM,\" Advik pointed out.

Chetana quickly defended herself. \"You replied.\".

\"That\'s different,\" he countered cheekily.

\"How?\" she challenged.

\"I am professionally nocturnal,\" Advik stated with absolute
confidence.

She rolled her eyes at his ridiculous statement, while he simply offered
a wide, triumphant grin.

As more time passed, a quiet, unspoken attraction slowly began to
replace their casual friendship. Neither of them formally announced it,
nor did they discuss the shift in their dynamic; it simply happened
naturally over time. It was evident in the way their eyes searched for
each other across crowded rooms, the way their conversations flowed with
effortless ease, and the way their shared silences never felt remotely
awkward.

Exactly three months after Chetana had officially joined the company,
Advik finally decided to take a step forward. He walked over to her desk
as she looked up from her laptop.

\"So\...\" Advik began.

\"So?\" she replied.

\"Would you like to have dinner with me?\" he asked.

Pretending to think it over very seriously, she asked, \"For free?\".

\"For free,\" he confirmed.

A gentle smile touched her lips. \"Then yes.\".

Later that evening, Chetana found herself standing before her bedroom
mirror for nearly twenty minutes. She was not usually the type of person
to overthink her clothing choices, yet somehow, tonight felt
significantly different than a casual outing. Eventually, she selected a
beautiful saree that perfectly balanced being elegant, simple, and
comfortable all at once. After taking one final, approving glance in the
mirror, she headed out for the evening.

Advik had arrived at the chosen restaurant a few minutes early to secure
their table. The moment Chetana walked through the entrance, he
completely forgot whatever coherent thought he had been forming. For a
long moment, he could do nothing but simply stare at her.

Noticing his intense gaze, she raised an inquisitive eyebrow. \"What?\".

\"Nothing,\" Advik replied quickly.

\"You were staring,\" she pointed out.

Recovering his wits, Advik claimed, \"I was conducting research.\".

\"Research?\" she asked skeptically.

\"Yes,\" he nodded.

\"On what?\".

\"Sarees,\" he answered smoothly.

Chetana sighed, affectionately calling him, \"Idiot.\". Advik just
smiled, having fully concluded that his research proved sarees were an
excellent invention.

The dinner date went surprisingly well, accompanied by delicious food
and even better conversation. They spent the hours sharing nostalgic
stories from their childhoods, laughing over embarrassing office
incidents, and discussing their deepest dreams and future plans. It was
the rare, beautiful kind of connection that only naturally occurs when
two people genuinely enjoy being in each other\'s presence.

At one point during the meal, while Chetana was passionately telling a
story, Advik quietly reached his hand across the space beneath the
table. Without any warning whatsoever, he gently pinched her waist.

Chetana immediately froze mid-sentence. Her eyes widened in absolute
shock as a faint, warm blush quickly spread across her face. She looked
around the restaurant frantically to ensure none of the other patrons
had noticed the intimate gesture.

Leaning slightly forward, she firmly whispered his name. \"Advik.\".

Maintaining a perfectly innocent expression, Advik asked, \"Yes?\".

\"Don\'t do that,\" she warned.

\"Do what?\" he asked, pretending to be completely oblivious.

\"You know exactly what,\" she shot back.

\"I have no idea,\" he lied smoothly.

Lowering her voice even further, she strictly warned him, \"Not in
public.\". The blush on her face became considerably deeper. Advik
simply looked entirely too pleased with his successful mischief.

\"You are impossible,\" she declared.

\"I\'ve been told that,\" he agreed with a satisfied grin.

By the time their dinner finally concluded, neither of them genuinely
wanted the beautiful evening to come to an end. Nevertheless, it was
getting quite late, so Advik safely drove her back home. They watched
the bright city lights drift peacefully past the car windows in a
comfortable, pleasant silence.

When they finally arrived outside her house, Chetana unfastened her
seatbelt. \"Thank you,\" she said warmly.

\"I should be thanking you,\" Advik replied.

\"For what?\".

\"For tolerating me,\" he teased.

She laughed playfully. \"I\'ll consider continuing.\".

\"Very generous,\" he noted.

\"I know,\" she agreed.

She opened the car door to leave but paused for a brief, hesitant
second. Before Advik could process what was happening, she leaned across
the console and planted a soft, warm kiss directly on his cheek. It was
a fleeting, tender touch, and before he could recover enough to say a
single word, she had already stepped out of the vehicle.

\"Good night,\" she called out, already walking toward her house.

Advik sat frozen in the driver\'s seat for several long seconds,
blinking rapidly as his brain attempted to process the unexpected
affectionate gesture. Eventually, a massive, ridiculous grin broke
across his face.

Nearly half an hour later, Advik finally arrived at his own home, that
same foolish grin still plastered across his features. However, as he
walked toward his apartment door, he abruptly stopped in his tracks.
Someone was standing right outside his home, waiting in the shadows with
folded arms and a highly serious expression.

Advik recognized his close friend immediately. \"Arjun?\".

Looking utterly exhausted, Arjun stated firmly, \"We need to talk.\".
