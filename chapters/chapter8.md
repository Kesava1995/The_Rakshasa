**Chapter 8: The Factory Massacre**

Two weeks had passed since the unforgettable, life-altering evening on
the secluded hilltop. To any casual observer, life had outwardly
returned to a state of complete normalcy. Advik dutifully continued
working his usual night shifts at the office, while Chetana continued
pretending that discovering her boyfriend was a shape-shifting Rakshasa
was just a perfectly ordinary, everyday life event. Neither of them
spoke very much about the terrifying transformation anymore, silently
agreeing that some profound truths simply required time, and some heavy
truths required quiet silence to be fully processed.

At around eight o\'clock in the evening, Advik\'s mobile phone suddenly
rang. The moment he saw the name flashing on the bright screen, he
answered immediately.

\"Arjun,\" Advik greeted.

\"Advik\...\" his friend replied, his voice on the other end sounding
utterly exhausted and broken.

\"What happened?\" Advik asked, his protective instincts instantly
flaring up.

There was a long, painful pause on the line. \"The police refused,\"
Arjun finally admitted.

Advik slowly leaned back in his comfortable chair, his expression
darkening. \"What do you mean they refused?\".

\"They explicitly told me to completely forget the factory,\" Arjun
revealed. A heavy silence hung between them before Arjun continued his
heartbreaking explanation. \"I filed all the official complaints. I
personally met with the senior officers, and I spoke to absolutely
everyone I could find in authority. They all say the exact same thing,\"
Arjun laughed, though the sound was painfully bitter and completely
devoid of any real amusement. \"The local MLA\'s political protection is
just too strong for anyone to challenge.\".

For a few tense seconds, Advik said absolutely nothing as he processed
the blatant corruption. Then, he asked very quietly, \"Where are you
right now?\".

\"Ranchi,\" Arjun answered.

\"I\'m coming,\" Advik promised firmly.

Advik\'s urgent flight landed in the city late at night. By the time he
finally reached his friend\'s house, it was already close to ten
o\'clock in the evening. Arjun looked far worse than the last time they
had met, with deep dark circles under his sleepless eyes and the heavy
burden of weeks of mounting frustration vividly written across his pale
face.

The two men sat down across from each other in the quiet room.

\"Show me everything,\" Advik requested.

Arjun tiredly handed over a thick stack of documents, including
photographs, copies of his police complaints, formal letters, and every
single piece of evidence he had desperately gathered. Advik spent
several minutes carefully reading through the paperwork before calmly
placing everything back down on the table.

\"They threatened the innocent workers?\" Advik verified.

Arjun nodded solemnly. \"They threatened me, too.\".

Advik\'s expression remained unnervingly calm, a stark contrast to the
severity of the situation. \"And the police?\" he asked.

\"They outright refuse to touch them,\" Arjun explained, as a bitter,
defeated smile appeared on his exhausted face. \"Apparently, basic
justice simply depends entirely on your political connections.\".

For several long seconds, Advik just stared thoughtfully at the
scattered papers on the table. Then, with deliberate purpose, he stood
up.

\"Where are you going?\" Arjun asked, looking confused.

\"To the factory,\" Advik replied evenly.

\"Now?\" Arjun asked in shock.

\"Yes,\" Advik confirmed.

Arjun frowned, his concern evident. \"Alone?\".

\"I am going alone, and I will come back as soon as possible,\" Advik
promised, his confident answer coming without a single moment of
hesitation.

At exactly eleven o\'clock that night, Advik stood quietly outside the
heavy factory gates. The massive industrial structure loomed
intimidatingly against the darkness of the night sky, while the bright
lights inside remained fully illuminated. Loud music echoed faintly from
somewhere deep within the compound, making it vividly clear that the
illegal occupiers felt entirely comfortable, highly protected, and
completely untouchable in their stolen territory.

Advik casually entered through the open front gate, and surprisingly,
absolutely nobody made any attempt to stop him. Several armed men
noticed his arrival almost immediately, and one of them aggressively
approached him.

\"Who the hell are you?\" the man demanded aggressively.

\"I want to speak to your boss,\" Advik stated calmly.

The man frowned deeply at the sheer audacity of the lone visitor, but a
few minutes later, Advik found himself being escorted deeper into the
heart of the occupied factory.

The arrogant head goon sat comfortably inside a large office chair that
clearly did not belong to him, surrounded by several of his armed thugs.
The pungent, overwhelming smell of cheap alcohol completely filled the
enclosed room. The leader looked Advik up and down with a dismissive
sneer.

\"And who exactly are you?\" the goon asked.

\"My name is Advik,\" he answered politely.

The goon merely shrugged, unimpressed. \"Good for you,\" he mocked.

Advik maintained his unnerving composure. \"This factory rightfully
belongs to my friend, Arjun,\" he explained.

The noisy room instantly became dead quiet. The goon\'s malicious smile
widened significantly as he processed the information. \"Oh, so you\'re
his little friend,\" he mocked.

\"Yes,\" Advik replied.

The goon burst out laughing, and several of his loyal men quickly joined
in on the cruel mockery.

\"I have come here to ask you to leave,\" Advik stated clearly over the
noise.

The raucous laughter only grew much louder at this polite request, with
one of the drunk men laughing so hard he nearly fell completely out of
his chair. The head goon dramatically wiped fake tears of amusement from
his eyes.

\"You came all the way out here\...\" the leader began, struggling to
contain his amusement, \"\...just to ask us politely?\".

\"Yes,\" Advik confirmed.

The goon laughed loudly once again, but then, his expression changed
instantly and violently. The mocking smile vanished completely, replaced
by hardened, deadly eyes. \"You go and tell your friend something,\" he
threatened.

Advik said nothing, simply waiting.

The goon leaned forward menacingly. \"If he keeps causing us trouble\...
I\'ll have him killed,\" he promised.

The room erupted into uproarious laughter once again, with several men
happily slapping the tables and others cheering loudly at the violent
threat. Advik remained completely still, observing their wicked joy. A
few tense seconds slowly passed.

Then, Advik nodded in understanding. \"I understand,\" he said.

The goon grinned triumphantly. \"Good,\" he said.

Behind Advik, several armed men quietly moved toward the room\'s exits.
The heavy metal doors were forcefully shut, and the distinct sound of
metallic locks clicking into place echoed loudly as the room became
completely sealed. The goon smirked wickedly at his trapped prey.

\"Now, it\'s your turn,\" the leader threatened.

Advik slowly and deliberately looked around the room, taking note of the
locked doors, the high windows, and the confident, armed men surrounding
him. Then, a small, almost amused smile appeared on his face.

The goon frowned in confusion. \"What the hell are you smiling at?\" he
demanded.

Advik simply looked up toward the ceiling, patiently waiting and quietly
listening.

**Then, it happened.**.

The entire factory abruptly went completely dark as the main power
disappeared instantly, plunging the massive building into absolute,
suffocating darkness.

\"What the hell?\" someone shouted in the pitch black. Several men
cursed loudly as panic began to set in. Mobile phones quickly appeared,
and bright flashlights were frantically switched on to pierce the gloom.
But then, inexplicably, all the electronic flashlights suddenly
flickered wildly before dying completely. The darkness deepened into
something absolute and total, bringing with it a strange, heavy silence
that settled over the terrified room.

Then, two massive eyes opened in the void.

They were a burning, unnatural golden color, and they were intensely
watching the men. Someone screamed in pure terror, realizing that the
glowing eyes were positioned far too high above the ground, far too
large in size, and burning far too brightly to belong to any human
being.

A deep, guttural growl rolled ominously through the darkness, vibrating
intensely through the solid concrete floor, shaking the metal walls, and
rattling the very bones of the men standing there.

Several goons stumbled backward in panic. \"What is that?\" one of them
cried out, but no one dared to answer.

The terrifying growl steadily became louder, ultimately culminating in a
deafening, earth-shattering roar that violently shook the entire
factory. Men desperately clutched their ringing ears in pain, some
collapsed entirely to the floor in shock, while others simply froze
completely in place. Every single primal instinct buried deep inside
their human biology screamed that they were in mortal danger---that a
lethal predator had arrived, and that death was imminent if they did not
run immediately.

Suddenly, the factory\'s power miraculously returned for one brief,
illuminating second.

**And in that split second, they saw the nightmare.**.

It was a gigantic, majestic lion, vastly larger than any natural animal
that should rightfully exist on earth. Immense, powerful muscles rippled
visibly beneath its thick, dark fur, while massive, razor-sharp claws
dug deeply into the concrete. Most terrifying of all were its glowing
golden eyes, which burned with an eerie, unnatural, and highly predatory
intelligence.

Then, the power vanished once again, throwing them back into the
unforgiving darkness.

Screams erupted frantically across the room. The first desperate scream
ended abruptly in a sickening crunch. The second victim\'s scream lasted
slightly longer before being silenced, while the third simply
transformed into a horrifying, wet choking sound. The sickening thud of
something incredibly heavy hitting the hard floor echoed loudly.

Another monstrous roar tore through the building, causing the remaining
men to run blindly in pure panic. They crashed violently into heavy
machinery, slammed into solid brick walls, and collided with each other
in their desperate bid for survival. One frantic man finally reached a
locked door, but as he fumbled with it, a massive, muscular shape moved
silently right behind him. He let out a blood-curdling scream, which was
instantly followed by absolute, chilling silence.

The power flashed back on for a fleeting moment---only a single,
terrifying moment---revealing copious amounts of fresh blood staining
the factory floor, but the giant lion was already gone. The lights died
again, and the merciless darkness returned, amplifying the spreading
panic. Some broken men openly cried in the dark, others desperately
prayed to whatever gods would listen, and several frantically attempted
to use their phones to call for help.

But there was absolutely no signal, and there were no answers to their
prayers. There was only the suffocating darkness, accompanied by the
horrifying sounds of wet growls, heavy footsteps, deafening roars, and
agonizing screams.

One by one, the terrified voices in the dark permanently disappeared.

The head goon stood completely alone, violently trembling as his earlier
arrogant confidence had long since vanished. The heavy firearm in his
hand shook visibly as he aimed it blindly into the void.

\"Where the hell are you?!\" he screamed, receiving no answer. \"COME
OUT!\" he demanded desperately.

There was only silence.

Then, another brief, flickering return of the electrical power
illuminated his worst nightmare. His eyes widened in absolute horror as
he saw the gruesome pile of bodies---his own men---scattered lifelessly
across the blood-soaked floor, with not a single one of them moving or
speaking. The lights died once again.

The goon\'s breathing became entirely frantic and ragged. \"Answer me!\"
he begged the darkness.

No one did. There was not a single human voice left, not one of his
loyal men remaining to protect him. There was only a suffocating, deadly
silence.

Then, the unmistakable sound of heavy, deliberate footsteps began
approaching him very slowly through the dark. The terrified goon
immediately raised his shaking gun. The oppressive darkness seemed to
physically shift as a gigantic, imposing silhouette emerged right in
front of him, and those terrifying golden eyes appeared once
again---much, much closer this time.

The goon frantically pulled the trigger, firing his weapon. The loud
gunshots echoed uselessly through the cavernous factory, but the massive
figure did not stop its relentless advance.

The power suddenly returned for one final, unbroken moment.

The towering, terrifying lion stood directly before him, its burning
eyes locked intensely onto his own. The goon completely froze in sheer
terror as the majestic creature slowly opened its massive mouth.

And then, impossibly, it spoke.

Its deep, resonant voice sounded exactly like powerful thunder rolling
through an ancient cave.

\"I am not a lion,\" the creature rumbled.

The goon\'s face turned a sickening shade of pale as the creature took
one final, deliberate step forward.

\"I am much more than that,\" the ancient Rakshasa declared, its lethal
teeth gleaming sharply as its golden eyes burned with absolute predatory
fury. \"And I am going to eat all of you up.\".

The head goon let out one final, blood-curdling scream of pure terror.

The power permanently vanished, plunging the bloodstained factory into
absolute darkness once more. And that final scream did not last very
long at all.
