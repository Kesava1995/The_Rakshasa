**Chapter 9: The Aftermath**

Two long days had peacefully passed since the sudden disappearance of
the armed men who had illegally occupied Arjun\'s factory. The shocking
news of the event had spread rapidly throughout the region like
wildfire. Dozens of ruthless men were simply gone without a trace,
leaving behind no terrified witnesses, no physical bodies, and
absolutely no logical explanations. Unsurprisingly, wild rumors
flourished across the city. Some people confidently claimed that rival
gangs were responsible for the violent massacre, while others spoke in
hushed tones of highly secret police operations. A few of the older
villagers, however, quietly whispered among themselves about something
far older, and far more frightening, than either of those mundane
possibilities.

Inside the busy district police station, however, the officers cared
absolutely nothing about village rumors; they wanted solid, tangible
answers. Advik sat perfectly calmly on a hard wooden chair across from a
visibly exhausted police inspector. A half-finished cup of cold tea sat
forgotten beside a towering stack of official files on the desk. \"Mr.
Advik,\" the inspector sighed heavily, leaning back in his creaking
chair. \"You arrived in Ranchi on the exact same day those men
disappeared.\". Advik offered a polite, agreeable nod. \"I did,\" he
confirmed. \"You also visited the factory that night,\" the inspector
continued pressing. \"I did,\" Advik answered evenly. The inspector
tapped his pen rhythmically against the wooden table, his deep
frustration mounting. \"And after that visit, nobody has seen them,\" he
pointed out. Advik simply shrugged his broad shoulders in mild
indifference. \"I cannot help you with that,\" he stated smoothly.

The tired inspector sighed once again. The entire questioning session
had been exactly like this: impeccably polite, maddeningly calm, and
completely useless for the criminal investigation. There was not a
single piece of tangible evidence linking Advik to the mass
disappearance. There were no fingerprints left behind, no terrified
witnesses stepping forward, no security recordings to review, and
absolutely nothing to build a solid case upon. There was only thin,
unprovable suspicion. The defeated inspector was just about to ask yet
another repetitive question when a sudden movement near the station\'s
entrance caught his attention. Several important-looking people had just
arrived.

Among the new arrivals was the powerful local MLA---the exact same
corrupt politician whose political influence had heavily shielded the
men illegally occupying Arjun\'s factory. The inspector immediately
stopped his interrogation and stood up respectfully. \"Sir,\" he greeted
the politician. The MLA acknowledged the officer with a curt nod before
looking sharply around the busy room. Almost immediately, his
calculating eyes settled directly on Advik. For several tense seconds,
neither man spoke a word. The MLA had spent the last two agonizing days
desperately trying to understand exactly what had happened to his loyal
enforcers. The mass disappearance made absolutely no logical sense; his
men were ruthless, well-armed, and highly numerous. They did not simply
vanish into thin air without a fight. Someone had done something
terrible---someone highly capable of effortlessly terrifying an entire
criminal gang. And somehow, every single investigative path kept leading
right back to this one remarkably calm man sitting in the station:
Advik.

The MLA\'s son, a young man in his early twenties, stood closely beside
his father. The exact moment he laid eyes on Advik, all the healthy
color violently drained from his face. His breathing quickened into
panicked gasps, his eyes widened in sheer terror, and he instinctively
took a stumbling step backward to distance himself. The MLA frowned
deeply at the bizarre, cowardly reaction. \"What happened?\" he
demanded. The young man could not even formulate a coherent answer. His
horrified gaze remained absolutely fixed on Advik, fully recognizing the
same face, the same piercing eyes, and the exact same man from a deeply
repressed trauma. His fragile mind instantly flashed back to a secluded
hilltop outside Pune---a pretty girl they had foolishly harassed, a
tense confrontation, and then the nightmare that quickly followed. He
vividly remembered the monstrous golden eyes, the razor-sharp claws, the
deadly fangs, and the terrifying man-lion that had violently appeared
before them out of thin air. For several agonizing months, he had
desperately convinced himself that the horrific event had merely been a
stress-induced hallucination born of fear and darkness---anything to
avoid accepting the impossible truth. Yet now, the man from that
unforgettable night stood right before him, looking very real,
incredibly calm, and intensely watching his every move.

The young man\'s hands trembled violently, a disturbing detail the MLA
quickly noticed. \"What is it?\" the father asked again, his parental
concern growing. The son swallowed hard, his voice barely rising above a
frightened, shaky whisper. \"Dad\...\". \"What?\" the MLA pressed
impatiently. \"I\'ve seen him before,\" the son finally confessed. The
MLA stared at his son in profound confusion. \"Where?\". The young man
hesitated for a terrified second before leaning in closer to his
father\'s ear, whispering a few quiet, frantic sentences. The seated
inspector could not hear a single word they said, and neither could
anyone else in the bustling station. But the psychological effect on the
powerful MLA was absolute and immediate. His previously authoritative
expression completely froze in place. The son continued his hushed
speaking, vividly describing the secluded hilltop, the aggressive
confrontation, the impossible physical transformation, and the majestic,
lethal creature that had emerged.

The MLA felt something icy and dread-inducing settle deep within his
stomach. Suddenly, the inexplicable fate of his missing men made
perfect, horrifying sense. It didn\'t make sense logically or legally,
but it made sense on a much darker, primal level. He slowly remembered
the ancient stories that the local villagers occasionally told---the
myths he had always arrogantly dismissed as superstitious nonsense. They
were ancient stories from the Puranas about powerful beings that walked
secretly among humans, and tales of lethal predators that did not
rightfully belong to the natural world. Slowly, with a newfound sense of
dread, the MLA turned his gaze back toward Advik. For the very first
time since entering the police station, he truly looked at the man
sitting before him.

Advik calmly met his gaze, his expression remaining perfectly blank and
completely unafraid of the powerful politician. The MLA instantly
understood the grave reality of the situation: there would never be any
physical evidence, there would never be a recorded confession, and
pursuing this dangerous matter any further would undoubtedly be the
absolute worst decision of his entire life. The bewildered inspector
looked back and forth between the two silent men. \"Sir?\" he prompted.
The MLA remained silently frozen for several long moments before he
finally found his voice. \"Release him,\" he ordered abruptly. The
inspector blinked in sheer shock. \"Sir?\". \"I said release him,\" the
MLA repeated firmly, leaving no room for argument. \"But---\" the
inspector tried to protest, only to be sharply cut off. \"There is no
evidence,\" the politician stated decisively. The inspector reluctantly
nodded his head, knowing that legally, the MLA was entirely correct;
there was absolutely nothing keeping Advik detained there.

A few short minutes later, the necessary official paperwork was
completely finished. Advik stood up smoothly as the inspector handed
over his personal belongings, formally informing him, \"You are free to
go.\". \"Thank you,\" Advik replied politely. He turned on his heel and
began walking toward the station\'s exit, where the MLA and his
terrified son still stood waiting nearby. Just as Advik reached them, he
deliberately stopped. The busy corridor immediately fell dead silent.
The trembling son immediately looked away, completely unable to make eye
contact, while Advik\'s intense gaze remained fixed solely on the MLA.
When he finally spoke, his voice was unnervingly calm and almost
friendly, which somehow made the interaction feel significantly worse.

\"Your people caused enough trouble,\" Advik stated softly. The MLA
swallowed hard and said nothing in defense. \"Arjun\'s factory belongs
to him,\" Advik continued, allowing the heavy silence to stretch
uncomfortably. Advik then took a deliberate step closer to the powerful
politician. \"Do not touch it again,\" he warned.

The MLA felt a deep chill violently run through his entire body. The
spoken words were not an open threat, but they absolutely did not need
to be. For one brief, terrifying moment, the politician thought he saw
something highly unusual flashing deep within Advik\'s eyes---something
unimaginably ancient and purely predatory. And then, just as quickly as
it appeared, it was completely gone, leaving just an ordinary man
standing before him, patiently waiting for an answer. The MLA slowly
nodded his head in submission. \"It won\'t happen again,\" he promised.
Advik studied the man\'s face for a brief second, feeling satisfied with
the genuine fear he saw. Then, he offered a perfectly ordinary, pleasant
smile. \"Good,\" he said softly. Without saying another word, he turned
and confidently walked out of the police station.

The MLA remained rooted exactly where he stood, silently watching until
Advik completely disappeared from sight. His son finally let out a long,
shuddering exhale. \"Dad\...\" he began. The MLA immediately raised a
strict hand to silence him. \"Don\'t,\" he commanded. The young man
immediately fell completely silent, as neither of them wished to further
discuss the impossible horrors that had been whispered just moments
earlier. Some dark truths were simply better left entirely alone.

Far away from the suffocating tension of the police station, Advik
stepped out into the bright, warm afternoon sunlight. Inside his pocket,
his mobile phone vibrated with a new notification. It was a message from
Arjun. \"Factory reopened today. Workers are back. Thank you,\" the text
read. A small, genuine smile appeared on Advik\'s face as he quickly
typed out a reply. \"Take care of it. I don\'t plan on visiting Ranchi
again anytime soon,\" he sent. A few seconds later, Arjun responded with
a cheerful laughing emoji. Advik calmly slipped the phone back into his
pocket, letting out a contented sigh. The ugly matter was finally over,
the factory was fully secured, and his dear friend was entirely safe.
And for now, the ancient Rakshasa could peacefully return to being an
ordinary, mundane man once more---unseen, unknown, and definitely best
left undisturbed.
