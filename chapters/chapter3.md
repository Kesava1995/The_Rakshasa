**Chapter 3: The Night Shift**

The vibrant, sprawling city of Pune welcomed Advik with a relentless
wave of noise and energy. Unlike the quieter coastal town of Dwaraka,
this city never truly seemed to sleep; the chaotic traffic continued to
move steadily even long after the sun had set. Bustling shops remained
open late into the night, and apartment windows glowed like scattered
stars across entire neighborhoods well past midnight. For most ordinary
people, this endless urban activity would have been entirely exhausting,
but for Advik, the nocturnal rhythm felt incredibly comfortable and
reassuring.

His first official week at Rolling Updates passed smoothly and without
any notable incidents. On his very first day, he sat across the desk
from his new team lead, Rajesh. Rajesh casually glanced over the
standard onboarding documents before looking up with a routine question.

\"Do you have any specific preferences regarding your shifts?\" Rajesh
asked.

Advik nodded immediately, not needing even a second to think. \"The
night shift, please\".

Rajesh blinked in genuine surprise. \"The night shift?\".

\"Yes,\" Advik confirmed smoothly.

Rajesh leaned back in his office chair, clearly bewildered. He was used
to employees spending weeks trying everything in their power to avoid
night duty; they would negotiate, complain endlessly, or even threaten
outright resignation to escape the late hours. Yet here sat a brand-new
recruit, voluntarily and enthusiastically asking for the exact schedule
everyone else despised.

\"Are you absolutely sure?\" Rajesh asked, wanting to be certain.

\"Very sure,\" Advik replied confidently.

Rajesh simply shrugged, deciding not to question his good luck. \"Well,
that works perfectly for me, since nobody else was ready to come in for
the night shift anyway\".

\"Perfect,\" Advik said.

Shaking his head in amusement, Rajesh remarked, \"You are a very strange
fellow, Advik\".

Advik offered a polite, knowing smile. \"You really have no idea\".

Over the course of the next several days, Advik comfortably settled into
his new routine. He would head to the office to work after sunset,
return to his rented house near dawn, and sleep peacefully through the
brightest hours of the day before waking up again in the evening to
repeat the cycle. In the office, nobody questioned his schedule much;
his colleagues simply assumed that he was a dedicated night owl.
Technically speaking, that assumption wasn\'t entirely wrong,
considering his hidden supernatural nature.

One quiet evening, after returning home from a long shift, Advik
unlocked the front door to his rented house. The very moment he stepped
inside, he stopped dead in his tracks. Something felt distinctly wrong
in the atmosphere of the room. Relying on his heightened senses, he
subtly sniffed the air and immediately detected a faint, unmistakable
scent of rodents. His eyes darted toward the dining table, where the
bowl of fresh fruits he had carefully purchased just the previous day
looked entirely devastated. There were half-eaten bananas, pieces of
apple scattered carelessly everywhere, tiny bite marks on the remaining
produce, and a disgusting trail of droppings leading away from the
table. Advik stared silently at the ruined groceries for a moment before
letting out a heavy, disappointed sigh.

\"Wonderful,\" he muttered sarcastically to himself.

He placed his bag down on a chair and spent the next several minutes
thoroughly inspecting the entire house, checking the kitchen, the
storage room, the bedroom, and the bathroom. By the time he finally
finished tracking and counting the intruders, his expression had become
incredibly unimpressed.

\"Twelve,\" he stated flatly. There were exactly twelve rats infesting
his new home---not just one or two, but a whole dozen. Frustrated but
maintaining his usual calm, Advik pulled out his mobile phone and dialed
the property owner\'s number to report the infestation.

The call connected after a few rings. \"Hello, sir,\" Advik greeted
politely.

\"Yes?\" the owner answered.

\"I think there is a significant rat problem in the house,\" Advik
informed him plainly.

\"How many?\" the owner asked.

\"Twelve,\" Advik replied.

A long, heavy silence stretched across the line before the owner spoke
again. \"How many?\".

\"Twelve,\" Advik repeated patiently.

Another pause followed, and the owner now sounded genuinely alarmed.
\"Twelve rats?\".

\"Yes,\" Advik confirmed.

\"You actually counted them?\" the owner asked in disbelief.

\"Yes, I did,\" Advik said.

The owner exhaled heavily into the receiver. \"Hmm,\" he murmured,
before offering a remarkably unhelpful suggestion. \"Maybe they will
just leave on their own\".

Advik stared blankly at his phone. \"They ate all my fruits,\" he
pointed out.

\"Maybe you should just buy new fruits,\" the owner countered weakly.

\"Sir,\" Advik said, his voice tightening slightly.

\"Hmm?\".

\"They live here now,\" Advik stated.

The owner sounded increasingly nervous, clearly giving up any pretense
of trying to help. \"Look, I will see what can be done,\" he promised
vaguely. However, his dismissive tone clearly suggested that he intended
to do absolutely nothing about the situation. The call abruptly ended.

Lowering his phone, Advik looked around the quiet house. In the shadows,
tiny, gleaming eyes peered out at him from behind the furniture. One
bold rat quickly darted across the kitchen floor, while another rapidly
disappeared beneath a heavy cabinet. He folded his arms across his chest
and stood perfectly still. For a few fleeting seconds, he considered
waiting for the landlord to act, but he quickly rejected the idea. It
was painfully obvious that the owner wasn\'t coming, and the rats
certainly weren\'t going to leave voluntarily. That left him with only
one viable solution---a solution that happened to be extremely natural
and effortless for someone of his unique lineage.

The room fell completely silent as Advik gently closed his eyes. Tapping
into his inherent abilities, his body began to shift and alter
effortlessly. His bones reshaped themselves, his muscles smoothly
contracted, and his overall height decreased rapidly in a matter of
seconds. Within mere moments, a small, unassuming house cat sat quietly
in the exact spot where a young software engineer had just been
standing. Because of his complete mastery over his powers, this
transformation required absolutely no physical effort, no deep
concentration, and no painful struggle; it was driven entirely by pure,
ancient instinct.

A rat cautiously emerged from beneath a nearby cupboard, instantly
freezing in terror as it registered the new predator in the room. The
cat simply stared back with unblinking golden eyes. For one profoundly
unfortunate second, neither creature moved an inch. Then, the primal
hunt officially began. The rat desperately turned and ran, but the cat
moved exponentially faster. Advik was a literal blur of agile fur as he
executed a flawless leap, resulting in a short, sharp squeak followed by
absolute silence. Eleven rodents remained.

The second rat frantically tried escaping through the kitchen, while the
third scrambled to hide behind a heavy gas cylinder. The fourth
attempted to squeeze its body through a narrow, dark gap beneath a
wooden shelf, but none of their desperate evasion tactics succeeded
against the Rakshasa\'s relentless pursuit. Hours passed steadily in the
darkness, and the house gradually became quieter and quieter until it
was utterly silent. By the end of the long night, Advik had
systematically hunted and completely consumed every single rat that had
dared to invade his territory.

As dawn approached, casting a pale light through the windows, the small
cat stretched lazily in the dead center of the living room. A moment
later, the familiar, seamless transformation effortlessly reversed
itself, and the feline form dissolved to reveal the young man once
again. Advik stood up and looked carefully around his home. There was no
sudden movement, no irritating scratching sounds coming from the walls,
and absolutely no tiny eyes staring out from the dark corners. There was
only perfect, undisturbed peace, finally. He casually walked over to the
dining table, picked up one of the few surviving apples, and critically
examined the tiny bite marks ruining its surface. He sighed deeply.

\"I still need groceries,\" he noted with mild annoyance.

For a brief moment, he considered calling the unhelpful owner back, but
he quickly decided against it. Trying to logically explain how a massive
rat problem had entirely and mysteriously vanished overnight would
probably create far more inconvenient questions than it would answer. As
the first bright rays of morning sunlight finally appeared outside his
windows, Advik securely locked his front door and headed straight toward
his bedroom.

The busy city of Pune was just beginning to wake up, which meant it was
finally time for him to get some well-deserved sleep. Like every other
being of his ancient lineage, the quiet darkness of the night had always
felt significantly more natural and empowering to him than the bright
daylight. When he woke, tomorrow would bring another beautifully
ordinary day in the city. At least, it would be as ordinary as life
could possibly be for a powerful, shapeshifting Rakshasa secretly
working as a corporate software engineer.
