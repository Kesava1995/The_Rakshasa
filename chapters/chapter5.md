**Chapter 5: The Factory Problem**

The joyous smile instantly disappeared from Advik\'s face, immediately
replaced by sharp concern. It was undeniably clear that something was
terribly wrong. Arjun, who was normally a pillar of calm and practical
composure, looked completely exhausted and defeated tonight.

\"What happened?\" Advik asked softly, his protective instincts already
rising.

\"Can we talk inside?\" Arjun requested, his voice strained.

Advik nodded without hesitation, unlocking the door and ushering his
friend inside.

A few minutes later, both men sat across from each other in the quiet
living room. Arjun remained uncharacteristically silent for several long
seconds, staring down at his hands, while Advik waited with practiced
patience.

Eventually, Arjun let out a heavy, defeated exhale. \"I have a
problem,\" he admitted.

\"A serious one, judging by the look on your face,\" Advik noted
carefully.

Arjun gave a dry, humorless laugh. \"You could definitely say that\".

\"What happened?\" Advik pressed gently.

Arjun leaned forward, resting his elbows on his knees. \"You know the
sugar manufacturing factory near Ranchi?\".

\"The one you have been tirelessly building for years?\" Advik
clarified, his expression becoming highly attentive.

\"Yes,\" Arjun confirmed.

\"What about it?\" Advik asked.

Arjun tightly clenched his fists, his frustration bubbling to the
surface. \"Some people have occupied part of the premises\".

\"Occupied?\" Advik repeated, frowning.

\"An illegal occupation,\" Arjun stated flatly.

Any lingering warmth from the evening completely vanished from Advik\'s
face. \"Explain,\" he demanded quietly.

Arjun nodded, running a hand over his tired face. \"A notorious local
rowdy-sheeter and his men forcefully entered the property about a week
ago\".

\"A whole week?\" Advik asked, surprised that his friend had carried
this burden alone for so long.

\"I honestly thought it would get resolved quickly through the proper
legal channels,\" Arjun explained, defending his reliance on the system.

\"It didn\'t,\" Advik concluded.

Arjun laughed bitterly, the sound devoid of any real amusement. \"Not
even close\". He looked away for a moment, struggling to contain his
anger. \"They brought dozens of aggressive men to the site. Innocent
workers got threatened, supervisors completely stopped coming to work,
and now some of my most loyal employees are utterly scared to even enter
the factory\".

Advik listened quietly, processing the calculated disruption of his
friend\'s livelihood. \"Have you gone to the police?\".

\"I followed protocol and filed an official complaint,\" Arjun replied.

\"And?\" Advik prompted.

\"They vaguely said they are \'looking into it,\'\" Arjun scoffed, the
deep sarcasm in his voice completely impossible to miss.

Advik raised a skeptical eyebrow. \"So, they are doing absolutely
nothing to help you\".

\"Pretty much,\" Arjun nodded grimly. \"They have strong political
backing protecting them\".

\"Whose backing?\" Advik asked, his tone darkening.

\"A corrupt local MLA,\" Arjun revealed.

That single revelation immediately explained everything to Advik. He
sighed deeply, recognizing the all-too-familiar stench of corruption,
unearned political influence, and violent threats. These were the
standard, toxic ingredients of a societal problem that should never have
existed in the first place.

\"What exactly do they want from you?\" Advik asked.

\"The land, the factory, the entire property,\" Arjun listed, shaking
his head in disbelief at their sheer audacity. \"I spent long, grueling
years building that place from the ground up. I painstakingly followed
every single rule, I paid every required fee, and I legally obtained
every necessary approval\". His voice grew louder, thick with righteous
indignation. \"And now some entitled criminal thinks he can simply walk
right in and take it from me\".

For the first time all evening, raw, genuine anger visibly appeared on
Arjun\'s usually calm face. \"I cannot accept that,\" he stated
fiercely.

The room became profoundly silent as Advik absorbed his friend\'s pain.
He completely understood why Arjun was devastated. To most people
passing by, it was just another industrial factory, but to Arjun, it
represented years of relentless effort, countless personal sacrifices,
and honest, hard work. Losing that factory would be like having a core
part of his identity ripped away.

\"What are you planning to do now?\" Advik finally asked.

\"I don\'t know anymore,\" Arjun answered immediately, the admission
tasting like ash in his mouth. \"I honestly thought the police would
handle it, but they haven\'t. I thought the local administration would
actively intervene, but they haven\'t. I thought basic common sense and
justice would prevail,\" he laughed bitterly again, \"but apparently
not\".

Advik leaned back into the sofa, his expression remaining maddeningly
calm. \"How long has this standoff been going on?\".

\"About a week,\" Arjun repeated.

\"Then you must wait,\" Advik advised smoothly.

Arjun blinked, entirely thrown off by the passive suggestion. \"Wait?\".

\"Yes,\" Advik confirmed.

\"You actually want me to wait?\" Arjun asked, his voice rising in
disbelief.

\"Just a little longer,\" Advik clarified.

Arjun stared at him as if he had lost his mind. \"My factory is actively
being occupied by criminals,\" he argued.

\"I know,\" Advik said softly.

\"My loyal workers are terrified for their lives,\" Arjun continued.

\"I know,\" Advik repeated.

\"And despite all of that, you want me to sit back and wait?\" Arjun
demanded.

Advik nodded slowly. \"For now, yes\". The mounting frustration on
Arjun\'s face was painfully obvious, but Advik continued in his
trademark calm, soothing tone. \"Listen to me. If we recklessly rush
into this dangerous situation blind, things can easily get much worse
for everyone involved. Maybe the administration will finally decide to
act if they are pushed. Maybe public pressure will eventually build
against the MLA. Maybe someone higher up in the government chain will
intervene on your behalf\".

Arjun looked entirely unconvinced by these optimistic scenarios, and
Advik completely understood why he felt that way. Still, he firmly
continued his advice. \"You have spent years meticulously building that
factory. Do not let your sudden anger make rash decisions for you now\".

The tense room fell into a heavy silence once again. After a long,
contemplative pause, Arjun let out a weary sigh. \"You always do this,\"
he observed.

\"Do what exactly?\" Advik asked innocently.

\"You manage to stay perfectly calm when everyone else around you is
completely losing their mind,\" Arjun pointed out, referring to Advik\'s
remarkable ability to handle pressure.

Advik chuckled softly. \"Well, someone has to be the calm one,\" he
joked.

For the very first time that stressful evening, Arjun finally managed to
form a faint, genuine smile, though it disappeared rather quickly. \"And
what if nothing changes?\" he asked, seeking reassurance.

Advik looked at his dear friend intently for several long moments before
he finally answered. \"If absolutely nothing changes\...\" he began, his
voice remaining terrifyingly calm and composed, \"\...we will figure
something out together\".

Arjun nodded slowly, absorbing the promise. The vague answer wasn\'t
exactly perfect, but coming from a friend as reliable as Advik, it was
more than enough comfort for now. A few minutes later, feeling slightly
lighter, he stood up from the sofa.

\"I should probably leave and let you rest,\" Arjun said.

Advik walked him toward the front door to see him off. As Arjun stepped
outside into the cool night air, he paused and turned back.

\"Thanks,\" he said sincerely.

\"For what?\" Advik asked.

\"Just for listening to me vent,\" Arjun replied.

Advik offered a warm smile. \"That is exactly what friends are for\".
Arjun nodded gratefully and walked away down the quiet corridor,
eventually leaving the building.

The empty corridor quickly became eerily quiet once more. Advik stood
there thoughtfully for a few long moments before stepping back inside
and locking his door. Despite his outward calm, something deep within
about this entire situation severely bothered his instincts. It wasn\'t
just because of the stolen factory, and it certainly wasn\'t about the
monetary value of the land. It was because of the infuriating,
repetitive pattern of human behavior. He despised arrogant people who
were protected by corrupt power---people who genuinely believed that
normal consequences simply did not apply to them. He loathed bullies who
foolishly mistook deliberate restraint for inherent weakness.

Shaking his head to clear the dark thoughts, he decided there was no
real point in dwelling on the violent possibilities just yet. Arjun had
simply asked for a friend\'s help and a sympathetic ear, nothing more.
And, ideally, there was still plenty of time for the frustrating problem
to resolve itself peacefully through legitimate channels. At least, that
was what the human side of Advik sincerely hoped would happen.

Meanwhile, hundreds of miles away near the city of Ranchi, the armed men
illegally occupying the sugar factory were drinking and laughing loudly
among themselves. Protected by their political connections, they smugly
believed they had already secured their victory. None of those arrogant
men knew that their reckless actions had already attracted the sharp
attention of something far more ancient and dangerous than the local
law. And unfortunately for them, unlike the slow wheels of the human
legal system---the Rakshasa would not remain patient forever.
