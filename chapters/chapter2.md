**Chapter 2: Leaving Dwaraka**

The desperate, piercing cries of the infant simply refused to stop.
Inside the crowded, echoing halls of the Jagannath Temple, hundreds of
devotees folded their hands in deep reverence as they moved steadily
through the long queue. The heavy air was thick with the scent of
burning incense and sandalwood, while temple bells echoed rhythmically
through the ancient stone corridors. Yet, safely held in the arms of his
mother, Bhavani, baby Advik cried as though the world itself had
terrified him.

\"Please, Advik\...\" she whispered softly, rocking him gently against
her chest, but the infant only cried harder. Beside them, his father,
Jagadish, looked on with visible worry, noting that the child had been
perfectly fine until the very moment they crossed the temple threshold.
Bhavani nodded helplessly, feeling the overwhelming weight of the
situation. This pilgrimage to Puri had been a sacred vow; after years of
earnest prayers and dwindling hope, they had finally been blessed with a
son. They had sincerely promised to bring him for darshan, and now that
they stood before Lord Jagannath Himself, their beloved child seemed
entirely paralyzed by an inexplicable terror.

As they approached the inner sanctum, Advik buried his face deeply into
his mother\'s shoulder, crying with such frantic desperation that
several nearby devotees turned around to look at the commotion. Jagadish
frowned, suggesting the baby might simply be hungry, but Bhavani knew a
hunger cry didn\'t sound like this sheer panic. The relentless crying
continued without a pause until the family finally stepped out and left
the temple premises entirely. Only then, beneath the open sky, did the
infant slowly begin to calm down. Neither parent understood why their
son had reacted with such absolute dread, and neither would remember the
exhausting incident as anything more than a highly unusual childhood
moment. However, somewhere deep within the child\'s mind, an ancient
instinct---far older than memory---had already awakened and recognized a
profound threat.

Twenty-two years later, in the coastal city of Dwaraka, Advik sat in his
room and stared blankly at his laptop screen. For a long moment, he
simply blinked, completely still, before reading the email from Rolling
Updates again, and then once more just to be absolutely certain. A
brilliant, victorious grin slowly spread across his face.

\"YES!\" he shouted, his joyous voice echoing loudly through the entire
house.

Within seconds, Bhavani emerged hurriedly from the kitchen, demanding to
know what had happened. Jagadish looked up in surprise from his daily
newspaper, and Advik\'s younger sister, Rachana, rushed out of her
bedroom asking what had just exploded. Instead of answering immediately,
Advik turned the laptop screen toward his family.

\"I got it,\" he announced, beaming.

For a brief moment, nobody reacted as they processed the screen, but
then Bhavani finally understood.

\"The job?\" she asked, and when he confirmed it, she immediately rushed
forward to pull him into a tight hug.

Jagadish\'s usually serious face broke into a rare, proud smile as he
offered his warm congratulations. Meanwhile, Rachana leaned over the
laptop dramatically, inspecting the email with a skeptical hum. Advik
narrowed his eyes at his sister, asking what her dramatic sigh meant.
She folded her arms confidently across her chest.

\"Not bad,\" she conceded. \"I honestly thought they would reject you
the moment they found out how incredibly weird you actually are.\".

\"I am not weird,\" Advik countered defensively.

Rachana immediately launched into her list of evidence, pointing out
that he slept during the day, preferred working late at night, and
easily ate enough food to sustain three people.

\"That is just called being healthy,\" Advik argued.

She nodded thoughtfully, a mischievous glint in her eye. \"Exactly what
a Rakshasa would say,\" she stated.

Advik froze completely for half a second, his heart skipping a beat at
her accidental accuracy, before he forced himself to roll his eyes.

\"Very funny,\" he muttered dismissively.

Rachana burst out laughing, blissfully unaware of how terrifyingly close
her casual joke was to the actual truth.

That evening, the family celebrated the good news together. It was
nothing extravagant, just a simple, hearty dinner shared around the
table, with Bhavani insisting on preparing every single one of Advik\'s
favorite dishes. Jagadish even returned early from his workplace to
spend the evening with them. The atmosphere in the house felt incredibly
warm, comfortable, and perfectly normal, which was exactly the kind of
evening Advik treasured above all else. Despite the heavy supernatural
secrets he carried hidden within himself, mundane moments like these
mattered deeply to him. Around his family, he wasn\'t a powerful
shapeshifter, nor was he anything remotely extraordinary; he was simply
a devoted son and a protective brother.

As they finished eating, Jagadish asked when he was scheduled to leave.

\"Next week,\" Advik replied, confirming he would be moving to Pune.

Rachana groaned dramatically, complaining that she would now have to
find a new hobby since annoying him was her primary source of
entertainment.

\"Find a real hobby,\" he shot back, to which she proudly declared that
he *was* her hobby.

\"That explains a lot,\" Advik muttered, making Bhavani laugh despite
her growing maternal concern.

Her expression soon softened as she reminded him that he would be living
all alone. Advik assured her he would manage, but she hesitated, gently
asking him to be careful about his unusual habits. When Advik asked what
she meant, Rachana eagerly pointed at him, thrilled that their mother
agreed he was weird. Bhavani ignored her daughter and listed her
worries, noting that he stayed awake far too late, barely slept normal
hours, and occasionally forgot his meals.

\"I never forget meals,\" Advik protested.

Jagadish chuckled, agreeing with his son by stating he had never seen
anyone forget a meal with Advik\'s level of unwavering dedication.

The entire family laughed together, and Advik shook his head fondly,
promising once more that he would be perfectly fine. His mother smiled
warmly, knowing deep down that he would be. She didn\'t trust him
because she understood the supernatural truth about him, but simply
because she had absolute faith in the responsible young man she had
raised.

Much later that night, after the rest of the house had fallen asleep,
Advik stepped quietly outside onto the cool terrace. The coastal town of
Dwaraka lay perfectly still beneath the silver moonlight, and a
refreshing breeze swept through the tranquil darkness as he gazed up at
the stars. Pune represented a brand new city, a promising new job, and
the beginning of a completely new chapter in his life. For most ordinary
people, this relocation would merely mark the start of a professional
career, but for Advik, it felt like something far more significant. He
couldn\'t exactly explain why he felt this way, only that a major change
was rapidly approaching.

Somewhere far beyond the dark horizon, destiny had already begun
shifting its weight toward him. Neither Advik nor a young woman named
Chetana had any idea yet, but in just a few short weeks, their vastly
different worlds were going to forcefully collide. Once that happened,
absolutely nothing in his carefully constructed life would ever be the
same again.
